About

The idea for PharmAlarms.com was first drafted when members of the Engineering World Health at Vanderbilt University found out that the residents and visitors of Nashvilleâ€™s community shelters struggled to manage and take their medications on time.

As it turns out, managing medication is a difficult task not limited to shelters and low-income individuals. As medical science grows at an ever-faster pace, so does the number of pills that are prescribed to millions of Americans.

PharmAlarms.com offers a simple solution to this problem. This application module is optimized to include only the most important information regarding medications: the name, characteristic, time and duration of administration. At a hospital or a pharmacy where the drugs are being dispensed, the staff can quickly generate a QR code to be scanned over the counter. The QR code can also be printed out and added onto a pill bottle or packaging to be scanned later.
